To be so strong that nothing
\ncan disturb your peace of mind
\nTo talk health, happiness, and prosperity
\nto every person you meet.<\/p>\n

To make all your friends feel
\nthat there is something in them
\nTo look at the sunny side of everything
\nand make your optimism come true.<\/p>\n

To think only the best, to work only for the best,
\nand to expect only the best.
\nTo be just as enthusiastic about the success of others
\nas you are about your own.<\/p>\n

To forget the mistakes of the past
\nand press on to the greater achievements of the future.
\nTo wear a cheerful countenance at all times
\nand give every living creature you meet a smile.<\/p>\n

To give so much much time to the improvement of yourself
\nthat you have no time to criticize others.
\nTo be too large for worry, too noble for anger, too strong for fear,
\nand too happy to permit the presence of trouble.<\/p>\n

To think well of yourself and to proclaim this fact to the world,
\nnot in loud words but great deeds.
\nTo live in faith that the whole world is on your side
\nso long as you are true to the best that is in you.<\/p>\n

Christian D. Larson, Your forces and how to use them<\/em><\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
