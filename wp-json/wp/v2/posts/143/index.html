When might I abide in such a place,
\nA place unaclaimed, by nature ownerless,
\nThat\u2019s wide and unconfined, a place where I might stay
\nAt liberty without attachment?<\/p>\n

In solitude, the mind and body
\nAre not troubled by distraction.
\nTherefore, leave this wordly life
\nAnd totally abandon mental wandering.<\/p>\n

Penetrative insight joined with calm abiding
\nUtterly eradicates afflicted states.
\nKnowing this, first search for calm abiding,
\nFound by those who joyfully renounce the world.<\/p>\n

There in these lovely gleaming woods,
\nWith joy that\u2019s marred by few afflictions,
\nI shall pacify all mental wandering.
\nAnd there remain in blissful solitude.<\/p>\n

In pleasant dwellings formed of massive stone,
\nAnd cooled by sandal trees beneath the moon,
\nIn woodlands wafted by the gentle breeze,
\nOur minds intent on bringing good to others.<\/p>\n

Those whose minds are practised in this way
\nWhose happiness it is to soothe the pain of others,
\nWill venture in the hell of unremitting agony
\nAs swans sweep down upon a lotus lake.<\/p>\n

The oceanlike immensity of joy
\nArising when all beings will be freed,
\nWill this not be enough? Will this not satisfy?
\nThe wish for my own freedom, what is that to me?<\/p>\n

Shantideva<\/em><\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
